How to use this (first time here? start here)
  1. Open Parameters and fill in the Diet village coordinates (the village you're feeding crop into), your player tribe, and your alliance's merchant bonus if it has one. Leave Max route spread at "Hourly" unless a village is too far away to route hourly, or you want to try squeezing out more total crop by letting routes fire less often with bigger convoys.
  2. Open Paste village information and paste in your Travian village overview (resource + merchants table, plus the "Currently building" list further down the page — copy the whole page). Click Parse villages. Skip this step if you opened a shared link that already came with villages built in.
  3. Open Trade office levels. Set a Default level to apply it to every village at once, then fine-tune individual villages if some differ.
  4. In that same table, fill in each village's Crop surplus/hour — how much crop it can spare on top of its own consumption. A village left at 0 sends nothing.
  5. If one village is meant to feed the others (a strong producer that relays crop onward through your other villages before it reaches Diet), mark it as the Source using its radio button. Leave none selected if every village should just send straight to Diet on its own.
  6. Click Generate routes. Each row in the result is one recurring merchant route: which village it leaves from, where it's headed, how much crop, how many merchants, how often it fires (every 1/2/3/4/6/8h), and when it departs and arrives. Arrivals at Diet are spaced so every hour gets at least one delivery of roughly even size, even if any single route only fires every few hours.
  7. Read the warnings below the table — they'll tell you if a village can't keep up with what it's been asked to send, if a relay was skipped for being too far or too costly in merchants, or if some of the source's surplus couldn't be placed anywhere.
  8. Use Copy link any time to save or share your exact setup — opening that link again restores everything, including the villages, without pasting anything back in.
